what is the use of soap?

Soap is a salt of a fatty acid used in a variety of cleansing and lubricating products. Household uses for soaps include washing, bathing, and other types of housekeeping, where soaps act as surfactants, emulsifying oils to enable them to be carried away by water.

how to make soap wha are its ingredientS?

Ingredients: 18.5 ounces Olive oil – moisturizing 12 ounces Coconut oil – for good lather 9 ounces Palm oil – for a firm bar 1 ounce Shea butter – moisturizing 5.8 ounces Lye – a.k.a. Sodium Hydroxide 13.5 ounces water

To make cold process soap, you heat the oils in your soap pot until they're approximately 100 degrees. Slowly add the lye-water mixture and blend the soap until it thickens to trace. After the mixture reaches trace, you add your fragrance, color, and additives and pour it into the mold.
